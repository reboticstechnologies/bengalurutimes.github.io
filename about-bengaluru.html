Welcome to Bengaluru

Bengaluru (Bangalore) is more than India’s tech capital — it’s a city of neighbourhoods, green pockets, diverse food culture and creative energy. This guide highlights why people move here, what makes it livable, and the interesting experiences you shouldn't miss.

1. Livability: Why residents stay

  • Neighbourhood variety: Quiet residential areas for families (e.g., Jayanagar, Whitefield suburbs), energetic youth hubs (Koramangala, Indiranagar) and planned enclaves.
  • Good healthcare & schools: Multiple hospitals, international schools, and research institutes.
  • Accessible nature: Parks, lakes, and nearby hill stations — easy weekend escapes.
  • Community spaces: Cafés, co-working spaces and cultural venues that encourage community life.

2. Interesting things to explore

  • Culinary tours: Street food in VV Puram, artisanal cafes in Koramangala, and microbreweries in Indiranagar.
  • Arts & music: Live music at small venues, Rangashankara theatre performances and contemporary galleries.
  • Markets & shopping: From antiques at Chikpet to modern malls and boutique stores.
  • Weekend escapes: Nandi Hills, Ramanagara for rock climbing, and local nature trails.

3. Getting Around

Bengaluru’s transport is a mix — private cars, buses (BMTC), metro corridors, and ride-hailing. Metro expansion is gradually improving city-wide connectivity; traffic remains a key planning priority.

4. Working & Building a Life

For job-seekers, Bengaluru offers roles across IT, manufacturing, startups and services. Co-working spaces and accelerators help early-stage founders. Housing choices vary widely by neighborhood and budget.
