Bengaluru — The Garden City that's also a Global Tech Hub

From leafy neighbourhoods and lakeside walks to bustling tech parks and rich food lanes — Bengaluru blends livability with opportunity. Explore local news, transport updates, real estate, companies and tech developments that shape life in the city.

Why Bengaluru is Livable
  • Green & Neighbourhoods: Parks, tree-lined avenues and quieter suburbs like Jayanagar, Indiranagar, Koramangala and Malleshwaram offer balanced urban living.
  • Climate: Mild weather for much of the year — pleasant mornings and cooler nights compared with many Indian metros.
  • Healthcare & Education: Quality hospitals and premier educational institutions make the city attractive for families.
  • Connectivity: Multiple arterial roads, rail (local suburban) networks, Namma Metro expansion and good inter-city links.
What Makes Bengaluru Interesting

Culture, food, start-ups and nightlife — Bengaluru has pockets of surprises:

  • Food & Cafés: From filter coffee to craft breweries and experimental eateries, there’s something for every palate.
  • Cultural Scenes: Music venues, art galleries and theatre groups keep the cultural calendar busy.
  • Startups & Innovation: A thriving startup ecosystem, accelerators and research institutions.
  • Lakes & Outdoors: Ulsoor, Hebbal, and the nearby Nandi Hills make for quick nature escapes.
